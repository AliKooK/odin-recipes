Chicken Teriyaki Recipe

This Chicken Teriyaki recipe is a quick and easy dish that brings the flavors of Japan to your kitchen. Tender chicken is glazed with a sweet and savory teriyaki sauce, served over steamed rice or vegetables. Perfect for a weeknight dinner!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for thickening
  • Sesame seeds and sliced green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce, mirin, sugar, garlic, and ginger to create the teriyaki sauce.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken to the skillet and cook until browned on both sides, about 5-7 minutes per side.
  4. Pour the teriyaki sauce over the chicken in the skillet. Bring to a simmer and cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  5. If you prefer a thicker sauce, stir in the cornstarch mixture and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until the sauce reaches your desired consistency.
  6. Remove the skillet from heat and let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  7. Serve the chicken over steamed rice or vegetables, drizzled with the teriyaki sauce. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.

This Chicken Teriyaki recipe serves about 4 people and is perfect for a quick weeknight meal. Enjoy the delicious flavors of this classic Japanese dish!
